The world is our playground

 When you look in the mirror, what do you see?
When you think about yourself, what do you think?
When you speak to yourself, what do you say?
Now replace this questions with these ..
When people look at yourself,what do they see ?
When people think about you, what must they think ?
When people speak to you, what do they say ?
🌻
Well ! Now there is no such major difference in these 6 questions but the matter of fact is that these 2 group of questions are representing two different perspective.
We in this social/digital world are trying to figure out people's lives through their feed and what they show us.
Basically we judge ourselves by our intentions and others by their actions.😊
For instance, if you are in a huge rush, and cut someone off on the freeway because you have to get to the hospital to care for a loved one, you will interpret cutting the person off as totally justifiable. You are not a jerk, in fact, you are trying to help someone! The person you cut off, however, will have no idea you are going to the hospital, and will likely think you are a jerk.🌚
So to really perceive someone as they perceive themselves, you really need to step back and take a look into their intentions and their situation. Don't think about the consequences or your perceived meaning of their actions, but rather think about what circumstances led them to act in that way..🌝
Tell me one thing about yourself that you think people have different perspective about it ! 🦋👇
. Nikita Dhiman Blogs

Travel broadens the mind

Into a long and flowing dress,
That left the scent on everything she touched,
Of nature's sweet caress,
Everywhere the girl did go,
The flowers would all bloom,
And she could chase the lonely feeling,
Out of every darkened room,
She could drive out all your sadness,
And cause a frozen heart to thaw,
She'd paint the sky pink every morning,
But nobody ever saw,
No one thought to thank her,
For the warmth upon their skin,
Or for chasing all their demons,
From where the night-time's breath had been,
So she thought she wasn't needed,
She could leave and they'd not care,
But they'd just taken her for granted,
Since her light was always there,
Because you never thank the ground,
Until you know how it feels to fall,
Or just how much you need the sun,
Until it doesn't rise at all.🌼💛 Nikita Dhiman Blogs
